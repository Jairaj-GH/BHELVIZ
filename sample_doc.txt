SECTION 1 — LEAVE POLICY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1.1 EARNED LEAVE (EL)
All confirmed employees are entitled to 30 days of Earned Leave per calendar year,
accrued at a rate of 2.5 days per month. Earned Leave can be accumulated up to a
maximum of 240 days across an employee's service lifetime. Encashment of Earned
Leave is permitted at the time of retirement, resignation, or death of the employee,
subject to a maximum of 300 days. EL application must be submitted at least 7 working
days in advance except in cases of genuine emergency. EL cannot be combined with
Casual Leave in a single stretch.

1.2 CASUAL LEAVE (CL)
Each employee is entitled to 8 days of Casual Leave per financial year. CL is not
carry-forwardable and lapses at the end of the financial year on 31st March. CL cannot
be taken for more than 3 consecutive days at a stretch. CL cannot be combined with
EL, Medical Leave, or public holidays to extend a vacation. CL must be applied for
and approved in the HR portal before availing, except in genuine emergencies where it
may be applied within 24 hours of return.

1.3 MEDICAL / SICK LEAVE (ML)
Employees are entitled to 10 days of Medical Leave per year on full pay. Medical Leave
beyond 3 consecutive days requires a certificate from a registered medical practitioner.
ML beyond 10 days in a year will be treated as Leave on Loss of Pay (LOP) unless the
employee has accumulated Earned Leave which can be set off. For hospitalisations
exceeding 7 days, the employee must submit a fitness-for-duty certificate from the
company-empanelled physician before resuming duty. ML is fully carry-forwardable up
to a maximum of 120 days over the service period.

1.4 MATERNITY LEAVE (MAT)
Female employees are entitled to 26 weeks of paid Maternity Leave for the first two
children as per the Maternity Benefit (Amendment) Act, 2017. For a third child or
subsequent children, Maternity Leave is 12 weeks. Maternity Leave may commence
from 8 weeks before the expected date of delivery and the remaining balance after
delivery. In case of adoption of a child below 3 months of age, the adoptive mother
is entitled to 12 weeks of paid Maternity Leave from the date of adoption. A
miscarriage or medical termination of pregnancy entitles the employee to 6 weeks of
leave with pay on production of a medical certificate. Maternity Leave is not deducted
from any other leave account.

1.5 PATERNITY LEAVE (PAT)
Male employees are entitled to 15 days of paid Paternity Leave on the occasion of
the birth or legal adoption of a child, to be availed within 3 months of birth or
adoption. Paternity Leave can be taken in one continuous stretch or split into two
parts. Paternity Leave is available for a maximum of two children. It is not
carry-forwardable and lapses if not availed within the stipulated 3-month window.

1.6 SPECIAL LEAVE FOR PERSONS WITH DISABILITIES (PwD)
Employees certified as Persons with Disabilities under the Rights of Persons with
Disabilities Act, 2016 are entitled to an additional 10 days of Special Leave per year
for medical treatment, disability-related appointments, assistive technology
procurement, and rehabilitation. This leave is non-carry-forwardable and separate
from all other leave entitlements.

1.7 LEAVE WITHOUT PAY (LWP / LOP)
An employee may be granted Leave Without Pay at the discretion of the competent
authority when all other leave balances have been exhausted. LWP affects the
employee's continuity of service for the purpose of benefits calculation. Continuous
LWP exceeding 30 days without approval will be treated as unauthorised absence and
may attract disciplinary action. Pro-rata salary deduction is applied for LWP days.

1.8 SPECIAL CASUAL LEAVE (SCL)
Special Casual Leave of up to 10 days per year is granted for:
- Blood donation (1 day per donation)
- Appearing in departmental or government examinations (duration of exam)
- Participation in national/state level sports events on behalf of the company or
  a recognized sports federation (duration of event + 2 days travel)
- Participation in disaster relief activities certified by a government authority
SCL does not count against the CL balance and is not carry-forwardable.

1.9 HALF-DAY LEAVE
Employees may apply for half-day leave (first half: before 1:30 PM; second half:
after 1:30 PM). Half-day leave is deducted as 0.5 days from the applicable leave
account. A maximum of 2 half-day leaves may be combined to form 1 full day of leave.
Half-day leave cannot be availed on days when the employee is scheduled for shift
duty beginning before 6 AM or ending after 10 PM.

1.10 COMPENSATORY OFF (COMP-OFF)
Employees who work on a declared holiday or on a weekly-off day at the direction of
their department head are entitled to a compensatory off day within 30 days of the
working day. Comp-off must be availed within 60 days of accrual and cannot be
carried forward beyond that. Comp-off is tracked in the attendance management system
and requires the approving manager to certify the extra working day.

1.11 LEAVE DURING PROBATION
Employees on probation (first 12 months of service) are entitled to only CL and ML;
EL does not accrue during probation. However, upon confirmation, EL is credited
retrospectively for the probation period at the standard accrual rate.

1.12 LEAVE COMBINATION RULES SUMMARY
Permitted combinations: EL + Public Holiday, ML + Public Holiday, EL + Comp-Off.
Prohibited combinations: CL + EL in one stretch, CL + ML in one stretch,
Maternity Leave + any other leave.

SECTION 2 — ATTENDANCE POLICY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

2.1 SHIFT TIMINGS
The following shifts are in operation across all BHEL divisions:

  Morning Shift   : 06:00 AM – 02:00 PM
  Afternoon Shift : 02:00 PM – 10:00 PM
  Night Shift     : 10:00 PM – 06:00 AM (next day)
  General Shift   : 09:00 AM – 05:30 PM (applicable to administrative staff,
                    HR, Finance, IT, and R&D divisions)

2.2 GRACE PERIOD AND LATE MARK
A grace period of 15 minutes is permitted beyond the scheduled shift start time.
Any arrival after the grace period is recorded as LATE in the attendance system.
Three late marks in a calendar month result in deduction of half a day's salary.
Six or more late marks in a calendar month result in deduction of one full day's
salary. Employees who are late by more than 2 hours without prior intimation are
marked HALF-DAY ABSENT. Employees who arrive after half of the shift has elapsed
without any prior leave application or intimation are marked ABSENT for that day.

2.3 BIOMETRIC ATTENDANCE SYSTEM
All employees are required to record attendance using the company-installed biometric
fingerprint and face recognition system at the designated entry/exit terminals.
Dual-swipe is mandatory — both entry and exit must be recorded. Failure to swipe
out is treated as an exception and requires manager certification within 48 hours
else the day is marked as a half-day attendance. Repeated failures to record
biometric attendance (more than 5 in a month) attract a written warning. Proxy
biometric registration (registering fingerprints for another employee) is classified
as a major misconduct under the Disciplinary Policy and attracts suspension pending
an enquiry.

2.4 FALSE ATTENDANCE / BUDDY PUNCHING DETECTION
BHELVIZ employs a multi-layer false attendance detection mechanism:
- Badge RFID log cross-referenced with biometric record
- CCTV correlation analysis for entry/exit gate passages
- Machine learning anomaly detection on shift-start and shift-end patterns
- Cross-check between canteen swipe records and attendance records

Any discrepancy between the badge log and biometric log on the same day generates
an automatic exception flagged in the system as EXCEPTION_BIOMETRIC_MISMATCH.
Employees with more than 3 such exceptions in a month are automatically escalated
to the Security department for investigation. Confirmed cases of false attendance
are treated as gross misconduct attracting major penalty up to and including
termination of service.

2.5 ATTENDANCE STATUS CODES (BHELVIZ System)
  PRESENT        : Employee recorded valid entry and exit biometrics within shift window
  ABSENT         : No biometric record; no approved leave on record
  LATE           : Entry biometric recorded after grace period but within 2 hours of shift start
  FALSE_PRESENT  : System or investigation has flagged the attendance record as potentially falsified
  ON_LEAVE       : Approved leave (any category) covering this work day
  HALF_DAY       : Employee present for less than half the shift duration
  COMP_OFF       : Employee availing approved compensatory off
  HOLIDAY        : National/State/company-declared holiday
  WO             : Weekly Off (as per roster)

2.6 ATTENDANCE REGULARISATION
An employee may regularise attendance for a maximum of 2 days per month where
genuine technical failures in the biometric system occurred, subject to manager
approval and confirmation from the IT/Security team. Regularisation must be applied
within 5 working days of the affected date. Retrospective regularisation beyond
30 days is not permitted under any circumstances.

2.7 REMOTE WORK / WORK FROM HOME (WFH) ATTENDANCE
Employees approved for WFH under the BHEL Hybrid Work Policy must log attendance
via the secure VPN portal between 09:00 AM and 09:30 AM. WFH attendance is marked
as PRESENT in the system once the VPN login and the mandatory morning check-in form
are completed. Non-compliance with WFH attendance procedures will result in the day
being marked as Leave Without Pay. WFH is not permitted for shift-category employees
(Morning, Afternoon, Night shifts) engaged in manufacturing or plant operations.

SECTION 3 — SALARY, ALLOWANCES & BENEFITS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3.1 SALARY COMPONENTS
The Cost to Company (CTC) for all employees is broken into:
  Basic Pay            : 40% of CTC
  House Rent Allowance : 24% of Basic Pay (metro cities), 16% of Basic Pay (non-metro)
  Dearness Allowance   : Revised quarterly based on All India Consumer Price Index (AICPI);
                         currently at 46.5% of Basic Pay (as of Q1 FY2024-25)
  Transport Allowance  : Rs. 3,200/month for Grade E1–E3; Rs. 4,800/month for E4 and above
  Medical Allowance    : Rs. 1,500/month (reimbursed against bills) for all employees
  Special Allowance    : Variable component to gross up CTC; paid monthly
  Performance Bonus    : Paid annually; linked to company PAT and individual PMS rating

3.2 NIGHT SHIFT ALLOWANCE
Employees working the Night Shift (10 PM – 6 AM) are entitled to a Night Shift
Allowance of Rs. 250 per night shift worked, in addition to their regular salary
components. Night Shift Allowance is not applicable for voluntary night-shift swaps
that are not part of the official roster. A maximum of 26 night shifts are payable
in a calendar month.

3.3 OVERTIME POLICY
Overtime is applicable to workmen-grade employees (Grade W1–W6) only. Officers and
executives are not eligible for overtime pay. Overtime is computed at 2x the hourly
Basic Pay rate. Prior sanction from the department head is mandatory before overtime
can be claimed. Maximum permissible overtime is 50 hours per month as per the
Factories Act provisions. Overtime worked beyond the sanctioned hours without prior
approval will not be reimbursed.

3.4 PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT SYSTEM (PMS) AND ANNUAL INCREMENT
The Performance year runs from 01 April to 31 March. Annual performance reviews are
conducted in April–May each year. PMS ratings are on a 5-point scale:
  5 — Outstanding  : Increment of 12–15% of Basic Pay
  4 — Very Good    : Increment of 8–11% of Basic Pay
  3 — Good         : Increment of 5–7% of Basic Pay
  2 — Satisfactory : Increment of 2–4% of Basic Pay
  1 — Unsatisfactory : No increment; eligible for Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

Employees on LWP for more than 90 days in the appraisal year receive a proportional
pro-rating of their increment. Employees under active disciplinary proceedings are
not eligible for any increment until the proceedings are closed.

3.5 PROVIDENT FUND (PF)
Employee contribution to PF is 12% of Basic Pay. Employer contribution is also 12%
of Basic Pay, split as: 3.67% to EPF account and 8.33% to EPS (Pension Scheme).
PF is deducted at source and remitted to EPFO by the 15th of the following month.
Employees may voluntarily contribute up to 100% of Basic Pay as Voluntary Provident
Fund (VPF) in addition to the statutory 12%.

3.6 GRATUITY
Employees who have completed a minimum of 5 years of continuous service are eligible
for Gratuity as per the Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972. Gratuity is calculated as:
(Last drawn Basic Pay + DA) × 15/26 × Number of completed years of service.
Gratuity is payable on superannuation, resignation, death, or permanent disability.
In case of death or permanent disability, the 5-year minimum service condition is
waived.

3.7 MEDICAL REIMBURSEMENT AND HEALTH INSURANCE
All employees and their declared dependents (spouse, children up to age 25, and
dependent parents) are covered under the BHEL Group Medical Insurance Scheme with a
sum insured of Rs. 5,00,000 per family per year. Maternity cover of Rs. 75,000 per
delivery is included. Pre-existing diseases are covered after a waiting period of
12 months from the date of policy enrolment. OPD expenses are reimbursable up to
Rs. 15,000 per year against original bills submitted to HR within 30 days of the
expense. Hospitalisation claims must be filed within 60 days of discharge.

3.8 LEAVE TRAVEL CONCESSION (LTC)
Employees are entitled to LTC once every two years for travel within India. The
entitlement covers the employee, spouse, and up to two dependent children. Travel
by the shortest route by air (Economy class) or rail (AC-II class) is reimbursable.
LTC must be availed in conjunction with at least 5 days of Earned Leave. LTC cannot
be encashed (except in the block year concession scheme under Income Tax provisions
for government employees, which does not apply here). Claims must be submitted
within 3 months of return with original tickets and boarding passes.

3.9 CHILDREN'S EDUCATION ALLOWANCE
Employees are entitled to a Children's Education Allowance of Rs. 2,250 per month
per child for a maximum of 2 children, applicable from nursery/pre-primary through
Class XII (up to age 20 for children with disabilities). This is a reimbursement
allowance; fee receipts from a recognised school must be submitted twice a year.

SECTION 4 — DISCIPLINARY POLICY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

4.1 MINOR MISCONDUCT (ATTRACTS MINOR PENALTY)
The following are classified as minor misconducts:
- Habitual late attendance (more than 8 late marks in a calendar month)
- Absence without intimation for 1–3 days
- Failure to follow the dress code or safety gear policy
- Negligence in maintaining attendance biometric records
- Disrespectful communication in writing to a peer
- Misuse of company equipment for personal use on isolated occasions

Minor penalties include: Written Warning, Withholding of annual increment for up
to 12 months, Fine not exceeding Rs. 2,000.

4.2 MAJOR MISCONDUCT (ATTRACTS MAJOR PENALTY)
The following are classified as major misconducts:
- Absence without authorisation for more than 5 consecutive working days
- False attendance / proxy biometric registration
- Theft, misappropriation, or destruction of company property
- Wilful insubordination or disobedience of lawful orders
- Sexual harassment of any employee or visitor
- Disclosure of confidential company information to unauthorised persons
- Fraud, forgery, or misrepresentation in company records or claims
- Physical assault or threatening behaviour towards any person on company premises
- Reporting to duty under the influence of alcohol or narcotic substances
- Sabotage of company equipment, plant, or production processes

Major penalties include: Suspension from service without pay (up to 120 days
pending enquiry), Reduction in grade or pay, Termination from service with notice,
Dismissal from service without notice and with forfeiture of gratuity.

4.3 DISCIPLINARY ENQUIRY PROCEDURE
Upon identification of a potential major misconduct, the following procedure applies:
Step 1: Department Head issues an order of suspension (if required for custody of
        evidence or to prevent recurrence) and a Chargesheet to the employee.
Step 2: The employee submits a written reply to the Chargesheet within 10 working days.
Step 3: An Enquiry Officer (not from the employee's own department) is appointed.
Step 4: Enquiry is conducted with opportunity to the employee to be heard, produce
        witnesses, and cross-examine management witnesses. Legal representation
        is not permitted during the enquiry, though a departmental colleague may assist.
Step 5: The Enquiry Officer submits the Enquiry Report within 45 days of appointment.
Step 6: The Disciplinary Authority reviews the report and may agree or disagree with
        findings, with reasons recorded in writing.
Step 7: A show-cause notice is issued to the employee before a major penalty is imposed.
Step 8: The employee's response is considered before final order is passed.
The employee has a right to appeal to the Appellate Authority within 30 days of the
disciplinary order.

SECTION 5 — SAFETY POLICY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

5.1 MANDATORY SAFETY TRAINING
All new employees joining the Manufacturing, Boiler Division, Transmission, and
Power Systems divisions must complete a mandatory 3-day Safety Induction Training
within the first 30 days of joining. No employee is permitted to work on the shop
floor or plant area until the safety induction is completed and certified.

Refresher safety training is mandatory every 2 years for all employees in the above
divisions. Employees who miss the refresher training in the due year are barred from
working in safety-critical areas until the training is completed.

5.2 PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT (PPE)
Employees in manufacturing, plant operations, and site roles are required to wear
all mandated PPE at all times on the shop floor, including: safety helmet, steel-toed
boots, high-visibility vest, safety goggles, ear defenders (in noise zones above
85 dB), and insulated gloves when working with electrical equipment above 110V.
Non-compliance with PPE requirements attracts a written warning for first offence,
a salary penalty of Rs. 1,000 for second offence, and suspension from shop floor
duties for 3 working days for third offence.

5.3 INCIDENT REPORTING
Any work-related accident, near-miss, or unsafe condition must be reported to the
immediate supervisor and the Safety Officer within 1 hour of the incident. Formal
incident report must be submitted in the EHS portal within 8 hours. Failure to report
an accident or near-miss is treated as a minor misconduct. In case of an injury that
requires medical treatment beyond first aid, the Safety Officer initiates an Incident
Investigation within 24 hours. All reportable accidents (as defined under the Factories
Act) are reported to the relevant government authority within the statutory timeline.

5.4 FIRE SAFETY
All employees must complete the annual Fire Safety Awareness module (45 minutes,
online, mandatory). Evacuation drills are conducted at least twice a year at each
plant and office location. Tamper with or obstruct fire safety equipment (extinguishers,
hydrants, sprinkler systems, smoke detectors) is a major misconduct.

SECTION 6 — TRANSFER AND POSTING POLICY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

6.1 GENERAL PROVISIONS
BHEL reserves the right to transfer any employee to any unit, division, department,
or project location in India or abroad in the interest of business. An employee
cannot claim permanency at any particular place of posting.

6.2 NOTICE FOR TRANSFER
In normal circumstances, a minimum of 30 days advance notice is given for transfers.
In cases of urgent operational requirement, transfers may take effect with 7 days'
notice. Employees posted to project sites of duration less than 6 months are treated
as On Deputation and continue to retain their parent unit for all HR purposes.

6.3 TRANSFER ALLOWANCE
Upon transfer requiring relocation, the following entitlements apply:
- Transfer Allowance: One month's Basic Pay (paid once per transfer)
- Packing and Transportation of household goods: Actuals up to Rs. 75,000 for Grade
  E3 and below; Rs. 1,25,000 for Grade E4 and above; Rs. 2,00,000 for Grade E7 and above
- Joining Time: 6 days for inter-city transfers within India; 10 days for transfers
  involving relocation beyond 1,500 km; 15 days for international assignments
- House Rent Allowance: Applicable at the new location rate from date of joining

6.4 HARDSHIP POSTING ALLOWANCE
Postings to defined remote/hardship locations (notified list published by HRD
annually) attract an additional Hardship Allowance of 10–25% of Basic Pay depending
on the hardship category (A, B, or C) of the location.

SECTION 7 — RETIREMENT AND SEPARATION POLICY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

7.1 SUPERANNUATION AGE
The age of superannuation for all categories of employees is 60 years. Employees
attain superannuation on the last day of the month in which they complete 60 years
of age. There is no provision for extension of service beyond 60 years for regular
employees.

7.2 VOLUNTARY RETIREMENT SCHEME (VRS)
Employees who have completed 20 years of service or attained the age of 50 years
are eligible to apply for Voluntary Retirement. VRS benefits include:
- Ex-gratia payment: 45 days of Basic Pay + DA for each completed year of service
  (subject to maximum of monthly Basic Pay + DA × 60 months or the amount payable
  for the remaining service years, whichever is less)
- All terminal benefits (Gratuity, PF, Leave encashment) are paid simultaneously
- CGHS / medical cover is extended for 5 years post-VRS at concessional rates

7.3 RESIGNATION
An employee seeking to resign must serve a notice period as follows:
  Grade W1–W3  : 30 days
  Grade W4–W6  : 45 days
  Grade E1–E4  : 60 days
  Grade E5–E8  : 90 days
Shorter notice may be accepted at the discretion of the Competent Authority on
payment of salary in lieu of the notice period shortfall. An employee on probation
must give 14 days' notice. Resignation once submitted cannot be withdrawn after
acceptance by the Competent Authority. Full and Final Settlement (F&FS) is processed
within 45 working days of the last working day.

7.4 TERMINAL BENEFITS ON RESIGNATION
Employees who resign after completing 5 years of continuous service are entitled to:
- Gratuity (as per Section 3.6)
- Earned Leave encashment (up to 240 days)
- Provident Fund withdrawal or transfer
Employees who resign before completing 5 years of service are not entitled to
Gratuity but are entitled to PF and EL encashment.

SECTION 8 — CODE OF CONDUCT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

8.1 GENERAL CONDUCT
All employees are expected to conduct themselves with integrity, honesty, and
professionalism at all times, including outside of working hours when representing
BHEL in any capacity. Employees must not indulge in activities that bring disrepute
to the company or conflict with the company's interests.

8.2 CONFLICT OF INTEREST
Employees must declare any financial or personal interest that may conflict with their
duties to BHEL. This includes directorships, partnerships, or significant shareholding
(>1%) in any company that is a vendor, customer, or competitor of BHEL. Annual
Conflict of Interest declarations must be submitted to HR by 30 April each year. Failure
to declare a conflict of interest is treated as misconduct.

8.3 GIFTS AND HOSPITALITY
Employees may not accept gifts, cash, vouchers, or entertainment from vendors,
contractors, or any business associates beyond a nominal threshold of Rs. 1,000 in
value. Gifts above this threshold must be declared to the Chief Vigilance Officer
and either returned or deposited with the company. Festival gifts collectively from
a business associate above Rs. 2,500 are also covered by this policy.

8.4 SOCIAL MEDIA POLICY
Employees must not post, share, or publish any internal company information,
financial data, project details, or customer information on any social media platform
without written approval from the Corporate Communications department. Critical or
disparaging posts about BHEL, its leadership, products, or employees on public
platforms are subject to disciplinary action. Personal opinions must be clearly
marked as personal and must not be construed as representing BHEL's views.

8.5 ANTI-SEXUAL HARASSMENT POLICY (POSH)
BHEL maintains a zero-tolerance policy towards sexual harassment in the workplace as
per the Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and
Redressal) Act, 2013. An Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) is constituted at every
unit/division with a minimum of 4 members, of which at least half are women and the
presiding officer is a senior woman employee. Complaints must be filed with the ICC
within 90 days of the incident. The ICC must complete its inquiry within 60 days of
receipt of the complaint. Interim relief (transfer, special leave) may be granted
to the complainant pending inquiry. Confidentiality of all parties is strictly
maintained. False complaints made with malicious intent also attract disciplinary action.

SECTION 9 — TRAINING AND DEVELOPMENT POLICY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

9.1 MANDATORY TRAINING HOURS
Each employee is required to complete a minimum of 40 training hours per financial
year, including mandatory compliance modules (ethics, POSH, safety, cybersecurity)
and functional/technical skill development programmes. Training hours are tracked
in the HR Learning Management System (LMS).

9.2 EXTERNAL TRAINING AND CONFERENCES
Employees may apply for sponsorship to attend external training programmes,
conferences, seminars, or certification courses subject to budget availability and
relevance to their role. Applications must be approved by the Department Head and
HR at least 30 days before the programme date. Post-training, employees must submit
a learning report within 7 working days and share key learnings with their team.
Employees who leave BHEL within 2 years of an externally sponsored certification
costing more than Rs. 50,000 must refund a proportional cost to the company (full
refund if leaving within 1 year, 50% if leaving in year 2).

9.3 HIGHER EDUCATION SPONSORSHIP
Employees who have completed 3 years of service may apply for sponsorship for
part-time or distance-learning postgraduate programmes relevant to their function.
BHEL reimburses up to Rs. 1,50,000 per year towards tuition fees for approved
programmes. A service bond of 3 years post-completion of the programme is required.

SECTION 10 — GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL POLICY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

10.1 GRIEVANCE DEFINITION
A grievance is any dissatisfaction or sense of injustice felt by an employee in
connection with their employment situation: pay, working conditions, leave, promotion,
transfer, disciplinary action, workload, or interpersonal conflict with colleagues
or supervisors.

10.2 GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE
Step 1 — Informal resolution: Employee discusses the grievance with their immediate
         supervisor within 5 working days of the grievance arising. The supervisor
         must respond within 5 working days.
Step 2 — Formal grievance: If unresolved, employee submits a written grievance form
         to the HR department within 10 working days of the supervisor's response (or
         non-response). HR acknowledges within 3 working days.
Step 3 — HR inquiry and resolution: HR investigates and provides a written response
         within 15 working days. HR may convene a Grievance Redressal Committee for
         complex matters.
Step 4 — Appellate authority: If the employee is unsatisfied, they may escalate to
         the Unit HR Head within 10 working days. Decision at this level is final within
         the company's internal mechanism.

10.3 WHISTLEBLOWER / VIGIL MECHANISM
Employees may report suspected fraud, corruption, or policy violations through the
BHEL Vigilance hotline (1800-XXX-BHEL) or the secure email (vigilance@bhel.in).
Anonymous complaints are accepted. The Vigilance Department investigates all
complaints and maintains confidentiality to the maximum extent practicable. Victimisation
of a whistleblower is a major misconduct.

SECTION 11 — IT AND DATA SECURITY POLICY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

11.1 ACCEPTABLE USE OF IT RESOURCES
Company IT assets (laptops, desktops, mobile devices, email accounts, intranet,
VPN, licensed software) are to be used primarily for official purposes. Incidental
personal use is permitted provided it does not interfere with work, consume excessive
bandwidth, violate other policies, or access prohibited content (gambling, adult
content, piracy, hate speech). All activity on company IT systems is subject to
monitoring.

11.2 PASSWORD AND ACCOUNT SECURITY
Passwords must be a minimum of 12 characters and contain at least one uppercase
letter, one lowercase letter, one numeral, and one special character. Passwords must
be changed every 90 days. System-enforced password history prevents reuse of the
last 12 passwords. Sharing of login credentials with colleagues is strictly prohibited.
MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication) is mandatory for all remote access (VPN, Outlook
Web Access, and all cloud-based applications).

11.3 DATA CLASSIFICATION AND HANDLING
BHEL data is classified into four levels:
  PUBLIC        : May be shared externally (press releases, published reports)
  INTERNAL      : For BHEL employees only; not for external circulation
  CONFIDENTIAL  : Restricted to specific departments or project teams; must be
                  encrypted in transit and at rest
  SECRET        : Defence-related or extremely sensitive commercial data;
                  access requires explicit authorisation from a designated authority

Employee attendance data in BHELVIZ is classified as CONFIDENTIAL. Access to
BHELVIZ is restricted to authorised users who have completed the security onboarding
process and received an approved BHELVIZ access credential.

11.4 CYBERSECURITY INCIDENT REPORTING
Any suspected cybersecurity incident (phishing email received, device lost or stolen,
unauthorised access to company system, malware infection) must be reported to the
IT Security helpdesk (security@bhel.in) within 1 hour of awareness. The IT Security
team will issue a ticket and commence investigation within 2 hours during business
hours.

SECTION 12 — TRAVEL POLICY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

12.1 DOMESTIC TRAVEL ENTITLEMENT BY GRADE
  Grade W1–W3 : Train (Sleeper/3AC), Budget hotel up to Rs. 1,200/night,
                Daily Allowance Rs. 500/day
  Grade W4–W6 : Train (2AC), Hotel up to Rs. 2,000/night, DA Rs. 700/day
  Grade E1–E3 : Train (1AC) or Economy air, Hotel up to Rs. 3,500/night, DA Rs. 1,000/day
  Grade E4–E6 : Economy air, Hotel up to Rs. 5,500/night, DA Rs. 1,500/day
  Grade E7+   : Business air (flights > 2 hours), Hotel up to Rs. 9,000/night, DA Rs. 2,000/day

12.2 INTERNATIONAL TRAVEL
International travel requires approval from Division Head and CFO for all grades.
Business class is permitted for all grades on flights exceeding 7 hours. Per diem
for international travel is as per the Ministry of Finance prescribed rates for
the country of travel. A travel insurance policy must be activated for all
international trips through the company's empanelled insurer.

12.3 TRAVEL ADVANCE AND EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT
Travel advances (up to 80% of estimated expense) may be drawn from Finance 3 working
days before travel. All expense claims must be submitted with original bills within
10 working days of return. Unsubstantiated or unsupported claims will be rejected.
Outstanding travel advances not settled within 30 days will be deducted from salary.

QUICK REFERENCE: COMMON POLICY ANSWERS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Q: How many days of Earned Leave am I entitled to per year?
A: 30 days per calendar year, accrued at 2.5 days per month.

Q: What is the maximum EL I can accumulate?
A: 240 days across the service lifetime; encashment on exit up to 300 days.

Q: How many Casual Leaves do I get?
A: 8 days per financial year; non-carry-forwardable; lapses on 31 March.

Q: Can I combine CL with EL?
A: No. CL and EL cannot be combined in a single stretch.

Q: What is the grace period for attendance?
A: 15 minutes beyond the scheduled shift start time.

Q: How many late marks lead to a salary deduction?
A: 3 late marks = half-day deduction; 6 or more = full-day deduction in a month.

Q: Am I entitled to maternity leave for a third child?
A: Yes, but only 12 weeks (not 26 weeks which applies to the first two children).

Q: What is the paternity leave entitlement?
A: 15 days of paid leave, availed within 3 months of birth or adoption, for up to 2 children.

Q: What happens if I have false attendance recorded?
A: It is classified as a major misconduct and can result in suspension or termination.

Q: What is the retirement age?
A: 60 years for all employee categories.

Q: What is the notice period for Grade E4?
A: 60 days.

Q: Am I eligible for overtime?
A: Only workmen-grade employees (W1–W6) are eligible for overtime at 2x hourly Basic Pay.

Q: What PPE is mandatory on the shop floor?
A: Safety helmet, steel-toed boots, high-visibility vest, safety goggles, ear defenders
   in noise zones, and insulated gloves when working with electrical equipment above 110V.

Q: How do I regularise an attendance exception?
A: Apply within 5 working days of the affected date; maximum 2 regularisations per month.

Q: What is the maximum night shifts payable in a month?
A: 26 night shifts. Night Shift Allowance is Rs. 250 per night.
